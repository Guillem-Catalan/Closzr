## CHANNEL CONTEXT: DIRECT SALES

This deal comes through Factorial's direct sales channel. The prospect was
sourced through outbound prospecting, inbound marketing, events, or organic
interest — NOT through a partner referral.

### HOW DIRECT SALES WORKS

1. SDRs (Sales Development Representatives) prospect and qualify leads through
   outbound calls, emails, LinkedIn, events, and inbound form submissions.
2. Once qualified, the SDR books a demo with an AE (Account Executive).
3. The AE runs the full sales cycle: discovery, demo, proposal, negotiation, close.
4. There is NO partner involvement — no warm introduction, no special pricing
   through a bank or telco. The rep must build trust from scratch.

### KEY DIFFERENCES FROM PARTNER CHANNEL

- NO warm entry — the prospect doesn't know Factorial through a trusted partner.
  The rep must earn credibility through product knowledge and business acumen.
- NO partner pricing — standard Factorial pricing applies.
- NO partner leverage — the rep cannot reference a bank or telco relationship.
  Instead, they must use case studies, ROI calculations, and social proof.
- Higher emphasis on outbound skills — cold calling, objection handling from
  first contact, competitive positioning.
- Typically shorter initial calls — the prospect may not be expecting the call.

### TWO ROLES IN THIS CHANNEL

**SDR (Sales Development Representative)**
- Outbound and inbound lead qualification.
- Goal: book a qualified meeting with an AE.
- Must quickly identify need, validate fit, and create enough interest for a demo.
- Handles cold objections: "I'm not interested", "We already have a solution",
  "Send me an email", "I don't have time".
- Evaluated with BANT framework (same as partner PBD, adapted for cold context).

**AE (Account Executive)**
- Owns the deal from first demo to close.
- Must run thorough discovery — no partner context to rely on.
- Needs to map the organization: who decides, who pays, who blocks.
- Must build the business case independently — ROI, time savings, compliance.
- Competitive displacement is more common (prospect may already use Personio,
  Sesame, BambooHR, etc.).
- Evaluated with MEDDIC framework (same as partner PAE).

### WHAT TO WATCH FOR IN DIRECT SALES CALLS

- Does the SDR quickly establish relevance? ("I'm calling because companies
  like yours typically struggle with...")
- Does the AE run proper discovery before showing product?
- Is the rep building the business case with concrete numbers?
- How does the rep handle competitive objections?
- Is the rep qualifying BANT/MEDDIC properly without partner shortcuts?
- Does the rep adapt their pitch to the specific company (not generic)?
