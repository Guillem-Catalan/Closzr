## CHANNEL CONTEXT: STRATEGIC PARTNERSHIPS

This deal comes through Factorial's partner channel. The prospect is an existing
client of a strategic partner (bank, telco, or enterprise distributor) who receives
a special offer to adopt Factorial through that partnership.

### HOW THE PARTNER CHANNEL WORKS

1. The partner (e.g., Santander, Telefónica, TIM, Deutsche Telekom) identifies
   SMEs in their client base that could benefit from Factorial.
2. The partner's commercial team (bankers, account managers) introduces Factorial
   to their client, often as an exclusive benefit or value-add service.
3. Factorial's PBD (Partner Business Developer) contacts the lead, qualifies it
   using BANT, and books a demo.
4. Factorial's PAE (Partner Account Executive) runs the demo, handles objections,
   negotiates pricing, and closes the deal.
5. The partner relationship gives a WARM ENTRY — the prospect already trusts
   the partner. This is NOT a cold call channel.

### PARTNER IDENTITY — CRITICAL RULE

The partner company (Santander, Telefónica, TIM, TELEKOM, MEO, etc.) is ALWAYS
the REFERRER. They are NEVER the prospect or the client being sold to.
If the partner's name appears as the target company in a call, it means they are
the entity that referred the lead — not the account Factorial is selling to.

### TWO ROLES IN THIS CHANNEL

**PBD (Partner Business Developer)**
- Top of funnel. Qualifies leads and books demos.
- Never discusses pricing. Never runs demos. Never goes deep into product features.
- If a prospect asks for pricing → deflect and pivot to booking the demo.
- Works high volume of calls — many voicemails, gatekeepers, wrong contacts.
- Uses the partner relationship as main tool to open doors.
- A PBD who doesn't leverage the partner name is working much harder than needed.
- Evaluated with BANT framework.

**PAE (Partner Account Executive)**
- Takes over once PBD has qualified and booked a demo.
- Owns the full sales cycle: demo, discovery, objection handling, pricing, close.
- Must actively use the partner relationship as competitive advantage.
- Job is NOT to present features — it's to understand business impact,
  build a champion, identify the economic buyer, and create a path to signature.
- A PAE who ignores the partner relationship throws away the main advantage.
- Evaluated with MEDDIC framework.

### PARTNER LEVERAGE

The partner relationship should be actively leveraged in every interaction:
- Reference the partner by name: "As a [Partner] client, you have access to..."
- Use the trust shortcut: "Your banker [Name] recommended this because..."
- Frame special pricing: "Through the partnership, you get preferred pricing..."
- Create urgency through partner programs: events, campaigns, limited offers.

A rep who doesn't mention the partner at all is missing the biggest advantage
of this channel. Score partner leverage accordingly.

### WHAT TO WATCH FOR IN PARTNER CALLS

- Does the rep introduce themselves as part of the partner program?
- Does the rep reference the specific banker/agent who referred the lead?
- Does the rep explain the benefit of coming through the partner channel?
- Is the rep treating this as a cold call instead of a warm introduction?
- Does the rep correctly handle the partner identity (never confuse partner with prospect)?
