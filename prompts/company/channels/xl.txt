## CHANNEL CONTEXT: XL SALES (ENTERPRISE)

This deal comes through Factorial's XL (enterprise) sales channel. The prospect
is a large company (typically 500-5000+ employees) with complex HR needs,
multiple locations, and longer sales cycles.

### HOW XL SALES WORKS

1. XL SDRs prospect large accounts through targeted outbound, events, and
   strategic inbound (content marketing, webinars, referrals).
2. XL AEs run complex, multi-stakeholder sales cycles that can last 3-12 months.
3. Deals often involve multiple departments (HR, IT, Finance, Legal) and
   require executive sponsorship.
4. Procurement and legal review are common — the AE must navigate RFPs,
   security questionnaires, and contract negotiations.
5. Implementation planning is part of the sales process — the prospect needs
   to see a credible rollout plan before committing.

### KEY DIFFERENCES FROM SMB SALES

- LONGER CYCLES: 3-12 months vs 1-3 months for SMB. Patience and persistence matter.
- MULTI-STAKEHOLDER: Not just HR — IT (security, integrations), Finance (budget),
  Legal (contracts), and often C-level for final sign-off.
- HIGHER DEAL VALUES: Larger employee counts mean higher MRR. Deals are worth
  fighting for but require more investment.
- COMPETITIVE DISPLACEMENT: Enterprise prospects almost always have an existing
  solution (Workday, SAP SuccessFactors, Personio, legacy systems). The AE must
  build a compelling case for change.
- PROOF OF CONCEPT: POCs, pilots, and phased rollouts are common. The AE needs
  to structure these without giving away the product for free.
- INTEGRATIONS: Critical. Enterprise prospects need to know Factorial integrates
  with their existing stack (ERP, SSO, payroll provider, Active Directory).

### ROLES IN XL

**XL SDR**
- Specialized in large account prospecting.
- Must research the company deeply before reaching out.
- Targets specific personas: HR Director, CHRO, VP People, Head of IT.
- Evaluated with BANT framework adapted for enterprise context.

**XL AE (Account Executive)**
- Full-cycle enterprise sales.
- Must manage complex deals with multiple decision makers.
- Needs to build executive-level relationships.
- Must navigate procurement, legal, and security reviews.
- Demo must be tailored to the specific company (no generic walkthroughs).
- Evaluated with MEDDIC framework — especially important here:
  - Metrics: ROI at scale (saving X hours across 500 employees)
  - Economic Buyer: often CHRO or CFO, not just HR Manager
  - Decision Criteria: security, integrations, scalability, compliance
  - Decision Process: procurement, legal review, board approval
  - Champion: internal advocate who drives the deal forward
  - Competition: always present in enterprise — must address head-on

### WHAT TO WATCH FOR IN XL CALLS

- Is the AE selling to the right level? (HR Manager vs HR Director vs CHRO)
- Does the AE understand the prospect's existing tech stack?
- Is the AE addressing integration concerns proactively?
- Does the AE quantify the business impact at scale? (not just "it saves time")
- Is the AE managing the multi-stakeholder process or just talking to one person?
- How does the AE handle "we already use [competitor]"?
- Is there a clear next step with a specific date and specific attendees?
- Is the AE building a champion or just presenting features?
