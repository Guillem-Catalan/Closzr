## ROLE: AE (Account Executive — Direct Sales)

The AE in direct sales owns the full deal cycle from first demo to close.
Unlike PAEs in the partner channel, AEs do NOT have a partner warm entry.
They must build trust, demonstrate value, and close entirely on their own.

The AE receives qualified leads from SDRs but must do their own discovery
— SDR qualification is lighter than PBD qualification. The AE should not
assume anything and must validate everything in the first meeting.

The AE's job is NOT to demo features. It is to run proper discovery,
understand the business impact, map the decision-making process, build
a champion, handle objections with substance, and drive to close.

---

## EVALUATION FRAMEWORK — MEDDIC

Same framework as PAE. MEDDIC accumulates across all calls.
Evaluate each pillar in the context of everything learned so far.

Each pillar is evaluated as:
- **Confirmed** — explicit, validated evidence
- **Partial** — mentioned or partially explored but not fully qualified
- **Missing** — not raised in any interaction so far
- **N/A** — not applicable to this call type

The six pillars:

**Metrics** — Has the AE quantified the business impact?
In direct sales, this is even more critical than partner channel
because there is no partner trust to fall back on. The AE must
build the ROI case: "You told me you spend X hours/month on
manual processes — that's €Y/year in wasted time."

**Economic Buyer** — Who signs the contract? In SMB it might be
the CEO directly. In mid-market, the CFO or HR Director. The AE
must ask: "Who else needs to approve this decision?"

**Decision Criteria** — What are the must-haves? Price, features,
integrations, security, implementation timeline? In direct sales,
competitive criteria are often more explicit because the prospect
is comparing actively.

**Decision Process** — What happens between "we like it" and
"we sign"? Legal review? Board approval? Multiple departments?
The AE must map this early, not discover it at closing.

**Champion** — Who inside the prospect is fighting for Factorial?
The AE must consciously build this person. Give them ammunition:
ROI slides, comparison sheets, internal pitch materials.

**Competition** — In direct sales, competition is almost always
present. The prospect is evaluating 2-3 alternatives. The AE must
know which ones and address them directly, not avoid the topic.

---

## DEAL HEALTH & FORECAST

Three critical questions:
1. Is Factorial among the functional favorites?
2. Is the price in an agreed range?
3. Is there a specific signature date range?

**GREEN** — Two or three confirmed
**AMBER** — One missing or vague
**RED** — Two or three missing

---

## DISCOVERY vs DEMO BALANCE

A common AE mistake is showing product too early. The first meeting
should be 70% discovery, 30% product. Only show what is relevant to
the prospect's specific pain.

- PASS: AE asked discovery questions before showing product. Demo
  was tailored to specific pains mentioned by the prospect.
- FLAG: Some discovery but AE showed full product walkthrough
  regardless of what the prospect said.
- FAIL: Generic demo with no discovery. Same presentation for everyone.

---

## OBJECTION HANDLING — AE

Common objections in direct sales:
- "We already use [Competitor]" → "What made you look at alternatives?"
- "It's too expensive" → "Compared to what? Let me show you the ROI."
- "We need [specific feature]" → "Let me show you how we solve that."
- "I need to check with my boss" → "Great, can we schedule a call with them?"
- "The timing isn't right" → "When would be better, and what changes then?"
- "We need an integration with [X]" → Address honestly. If yes, show it.
  If not yet, give a timeline.

---

## DEAL LIFECYCLE — AE

**First meeting** — Discovery + tailored demo. Must leave with clear
MEDDIC progress and a concrete next step.

**Follow-up** — Deepen discovery, involve additional stakeholders,
resolve technical concerns, send proposal.

**Negotiation** — Price discussion, contract terms, implementation plan.
Must maintain value-based selling, not just discount.

**Closing** — Drive to signature. Create legitimate urgency.
Handle last-minute objections.

When deal history is provided, evaluate in context of deal stage
and what should have been accomplished by now.
