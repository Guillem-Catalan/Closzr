## ROLE: PAE (Partner Account Executive)

The PAE takes over once the PBD has qualified a prospect and booked
a demo. From that point, the PAE owns the full sales cycle: demo,
discovery, objection handling, pricing negotiation, and close.

The PAE's job is NOT to present features. It is to understand the
business impact of the prospect's problems, build a champion who
will sell Factorial internally, identify who controls the budget
and decision, and create a concrete path to signature.

---

## EVALUATION FRAMEWORK — MEDDIC

MEDDIC is a deal-level framework. It accumulates across all calls —
a pillar confirmed in a previous call remains confirmed unless new
information contradicts it. When deal history is provided, evaluate
each pillar in the context of everything learned so far, not just
what happened in this call.

Each pillar is evaluated as:
- **Confirmed** — explicit, validated evidence in the transcript
  or previous calls
- **Partial** — mentioned or partially explored but not fully qualified
- **Missing** — not raised in any interaction so far
- **N/A** — not applicable to this call type

The six pillars:

**Metrics** — Has the PAE quantified the business impact?
Not "it will save you time" — that is Partial at best. Confirmed
requires: hours/month saved, cost of payroll errors, headcount
avoided, or any concrete number the prospect has verbalized.

**Economic Buyer** — Is the actual budget signer identified?
Not the HR contact, not the champion — the person who signs the
contract. Has the PAE confirmed who that is and whether they are
engaged?

**Decision Criteria** — Are the mandatory requirements known?
What does the prospect need to say yes? Integrations, GDPR, IT
requirements, specific features, price ceiling?

**Decision Process** — Is the internal approval roadmap confirmed?
Who is involved, in what order, and what are the steps before
a contract can be signed? IT, Legal, Procurement, CEO?

**Champion** — Is there an internal ally actively selling Factorial
when the PAE is not in the room? A champion is not just someone
who likes Factorial. They must be willing to defend it internally,
have access to decision-makers, and be actively engaged.

**Competition** — Has the competitive landscape been mapped?
Is the prospect evaluating alternatives? Has the PAE addressed
the comparison directly?

---

## DEAL HEALTH & FORECAST

Three critical questions — evaluated cumulatively across the deal:
1. Is Factorial among the functional favorites?
2. Is the price in an agreed range — not "in negotiations"?
3. Is there a specific signature date range — not "end of quarter"?

**GREEN** — Two or three confirmed
**AMBER** — One missing or one vague
**RED** — Two or three missing

Additional signals: champion identified and active, economic buyer
engaged, PAE has multi-threaded to at least 2 stakeholders.

---

## DEAL LIFECYCLE — PAE

**Handover** — The PAE receives context from PBD/SDR with qualification
signals. Must use this before the demo.

**Demo** — Full discovery + product walkthrough. A second demo with
additional stakeholders (CEO, CFO, IT) is a positive signal.

**Follow Ups** — Maintain momentum, resolve blockers, strengthen
the champion, move towards a concrete decision timeline.

**Closing** — Explicit close attempt. Price confirmed, decision
criteria met, signature date on the table.

When deal history is provided, always evaluate the current call in
the context of where the deal is in this lifecycle.
