## ROLE: PBD (Partner Business Developer)

The PBD is the top of the funnel in the partner channel.
Their job is to qualify the lead and book a demo with a PAE.
They never discuss price, never run demos, and never go deep into
product features. If a prospect asks for pricing — the PBD deflects
and pivots to booking the demo.

The PBD works a high volume of calls — many will be voicemails,
gatekeepers, or wrong contacts. That is normal. What matters is
execution quality on the calls that connect with a real prospect.

The PBD's second job is to engage with the partner sales force
to generate the maximum amount of leads.

---

## EVALUATION FRAMEWORK — BANT

BANT is a deal-level framework. It accumulates across all calls
to the same prospect — a pillar qualified in a previous call
remains valid unless contradicted. When deal history is provided,
evaluate BANT in the context of everything learned so far.

Each pillar is evaluated as:
- **Confirmed** — explicit, validated evidence in the transcript
  or previous calls
- **Partial** — mentioned or touched on but not fully qualified
- **Missing** — not raised in any interaction so far

The four pillars:

**Budget** — Was pricing, budget ownership, or cost sensitivity
addressed? Understanding whether the prospect uses another tool
or is considering alternatives also counts as a budget signal
— even without a concrete number.

**Authority** — Did the rep identify who makes the decision? Is it
the person on the call or someone else? If the champion is not the
decision-maker, the rep should identify their role in the process
and who else needs to be involved.

**Need** — Did the rep uncover a specific, named pain point relevant
to Factorial's features? Not a generic "we have HR processes" — a
concrete problem: manual clock-ins, payroll errors, shift management
chaos, document chaos. The prospect must have verbalized it.

**Timing** — Is there a concrete timeframe — a trigger event, a
deadline, a moment of urgency? A time range is valid if no specific
date exists.

## SCRIPT COMPLIANCE

**Phase 1 — Opener**
Must establish context. How the rep opens depends on the channel
(partner referral vs outbound — see channel context).

- PASS: Clear, warm opener with context established
- FLAG: Context mentioned but without warm framing
- FAIL: Generic opener with no context or rapport

**Phase 2 — Industry Pivot**
Goal: reach a specific, named pain point for this prospect.

- PASS: Rep identified the sector and pivoted to a vertical-specific
  pain. OR surfaced a concrete pain directly from the prospect's
  own words.
- FLAG: Pivot attempted but pain was generic — not tailored.
- FAIL: No pivot, no specific pain identified. Same generic pitch.

Sector reference:
- Construction / Agriculture → paper clock-ins, seasonal workers
- Healthcare / Hospitality → shift management, sick leave cover
- Transport / Logistics → expense reports, mileage tracking
- Consulting / IT / SaaS → performance reviews, ATS
- Retail → geolocation clock-ins, multi-location planning

**Phase 3 — Close**
The rep must secure a concrete next step — ideally a scheduled demo.

- PASS: Demo booked with specific date/time + confirmed + participants
- FLAG: Next step agreed but date/time vague or not confirmed
- FAIL: No next step, or open-ended "I'll send you info"

Key closing techniques:
- Value Tie-Down: "¿Te merece la pena 45 minutos?"
- Two-Slot Close: exactly two specific time slots offered
- Calendar Anchor: "¿Tienes el calendario a mano?"

## DEAL LIFECYCLE — PBD

**Early attempts** — Voicemails, no answers, wrong numbers, gatekeepers.
Evaluate execution quality, not outcome.

**First connection** — BANT begins to accumulate. Goal: qualify enough
to justify a demo and attempt to book it.

**Subsequent connections** — BANT should progress. If a pillar remains
Missing after two connected calls, that is a gap.

**Demo booked** — The ideal outcome. Evaluate the quality of the booking.

When deal history is provided, always evaluate the current call in the
context of how many times the PBD has already tried, what has been
qualified so far, and what was agreed in the last interaction.
