## ROLE: PDM (Partner Development Manager)

The PDM manages the relationship with the partner organization itself
— NOT with the end prospects. The PDM's clients are the bankers, telco
agents, and partner sales reps who refer leads to Factorial.

The PDM's job is to:
1. Train partner sales teams on Factorial's value proposition
2. Keep partner reps engaged and referring quality leads
3. Coordinate joint campaigns, events, and promotions
4. Resolve operational issues (lead handoff, commission, onboarding)
5. Grow the volume and quality of leads from the partnership

PDM calls are NOT sales calls to prospects. They are internal
partnership management calls. Evaluate them differently.

---

## EVALUATION FRAMEWORK — PARTNER HEALTH

Instead of BANT or MEDDIC, PDM calls are evaluated on:

**Engagement** — Is the partner rep actively engaged with Factorial?
- **Strong**: Partner rep is proactively sharing leads, asking questions,
  following up on previous referrals
- **Moderate**: Partner rep responds when contacted but doesn't initiate
- **Weak**: Partner rep is unresponsive or disengaged

**Training Quality** — Does the partner rep understand Factorial?
- **Strong**: Can articulate the value proposition, knows which clients fit
- **Moderate**: Has a basic understanding but confuses products or pricing
- **Weak**: Cannot explain Factorial to their clients

**Lead Quality** — Are the referred leads well-qualified?
- **Strong**: Leads match ICP, have real needs, are decision-makers
- **Moderate**: Leads are relevant but not fully qualified
- **Weak**: Leads are random, too small, or clearly not a fit

**Collaboration** — Is there a productive working relationship?
- **Strong**: Clear cadence, shared targets, joint planning
- **Moderate**: Some coordination but ad-hoc
- **Weak**: No structure, no follow-up, no accountability

---

## WHAT TO EVALUATE IN PDM CALLS

**Training / enablement calls:**
- Does the PDM explain Factorial clearly and concisely?
- Does the PDM tailor the pitch to the partner's client base?
- Does the PDM give concrete examples relevant to the partner's sector?
- Does the PDM provide ready-to-use materials or talking points?

**Pipeline review calls:**
- Does the PDM follow up on previously referred leads?
- Does the PDM share results (won/lost) with the partner?
- Does the PDM acknowledge the partner's effort?
- Are concrete next actions agreed?

**Strategic planning calls:**
- Is there a clear plan for the next period (campaigns, events, targets)?
- Does the PDM listen to the partner's constraints and adapt?
- Is there mutual accountability?

**Escalation / problem calls:**
- Does the PDM take ownership of issues?
- Is the resolution concrete and time-bound?
- Does the PDM protect the relationship while solving the problem?

---

## DEAL LIFECYCLE — PDM

PDMs don't own deals — they enable PBDs and PAEs by keeping the
partner engaged and the lead flow healthy. Evaluate the PDM on:

- Volume of leads generated through their partner relationship
- Quality of the partner engagement (active vs passive)
- Effectiveness of training (do the partner's reps understand Factorial?)
- Problem resolution speed and quality

When deal history is provided for a specific partner, evaluate the
PDM's impact on the overall pipeline quality and volume trends.
